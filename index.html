Jinglei Liang

I am a research scientist working on robot learning, 3D perception, and embodied AI at an industry research lab. My work is about giving robots dependable perception and manipulation skills that hold up outside the lab — estimating the physical world (shape, color, contact, success/failure) from cameras, and turning language and demonstrations into robot actions. Most of my projects pair a carefully engineered classical baseline with a learned model, and are validated on real hardware under conditions the model never saw during training.

Research

I am interested in robotic manipulation, embodied perception, and the systems that connect them. A recurring theme is robustness: methods that keep working under lighting changes, novel objects, and real-world noise. Selected projects are highlighted.

Cross-Illumination Grasp Verification from Fingertip Cameras
Jinglei Liang
Technical Report, 2026
code

A lightweight binary classifier decides whether a grasp succeeded or failed directly from in-hand fingertip camera views. I compare a calibrated computer-vision baseline against a fine-tuned MobileNetV3 classifier, reaching 99.7% accuracy under normal lighting and, crucially, retaining accuracy on a held-out low-light regime never seen in training — a cross-illumination evaluation that exposes the brittleness of threshold-based baselines.

Open-Vocabulary Multi-Camera Instance Segmentation for Bin Picking
Jinglei Liang
Technical Report, 2026
code

A promptable segmentation pipeline that fuses four calibrated RGB-D viewpoints to segment arbitrary, previously unseen objects in a cluttered bin. By combining multi-view geometry with open-vocabulary masks, the system isolates individual items for grasping without any per-object training, handling dense clutter and partial occlusion across viewpoints.

Language-Conditioned Manipulation with Diffusion and CVAE Policies
Jinglei Liang
Technical Report, 2026
code

Mapping free-form natural-language instructions to robot trajectories. I study two generative action policies — a conditional VAE and a diffusion policy — both conditioned on embeddings from a large multimodal language model, and pair them with a motion-retargeting stage that transfers human-demonstrated motions onto the robot. The result executes language commands end-to-end on a real arm.

A Minimal, Reproducible Toolkit for Robot Hand–Eye Calibration
Jinglei Liang
Open-source toolkit, 2026
code

A compact end-to-end implementation of camera intrinsics and hand–eye extrinsics calibration, covering both eye-on-base (fixed, third-person) and eye-on-hand (wrist-mounted) configurations via the classic AX = YB formulation. A real-time 3D viewer overlays the estimated camera pose on the robot model, making it easy to port any camera-plus-arm setup and verify the result at a glance.

Robust Object Color Recognition under Specular Highlights
Jinglei Liang
Technical Report, 2026
code

A real-time perception module that localizes a target object with Hough-circle detection and names its color from the dominant hue in HSV space. Specular-highlight rejection and a median-band sampling scheme keep the estimate stable under glare, and temporal debouncing suppresses flicker before the color label is published to downstream tasks.
